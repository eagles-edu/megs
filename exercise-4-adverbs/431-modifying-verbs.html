4.3.1 Modifying Verbs

  • 4.2.3 Positioning Adverbs (Part 3)
  • 4.3.2 Modifying Adjectives (Part 1)

The following adverbs are modifiers that modify verbs in the sentences:

  • accidentally, badly, carefully, deeply, easily, fast, freely, greatly, low, suddenly, well

Place each one in the correct position in the sentence. Two modifiers are required for the last sentence.

Click to check your answers.

Example. We walked as the sky got darker.(quickly)

Example. We walked as the sky got darker.(quickly)

We walked quickly as the sky got darker.

1. We watched a plane flying over the sea.

1. We watched a plane flying over the sea.

1. We watched an a plane flying low over the sea.

2. We encourage the victims to talk about their experiences.

2. We encourage the victims to talk about their experiences.

2. We encourage the victims to talk freely about their experiences.

3. She seldom chooses her words when speaking about other people.

3. She seldom chooses her words when speaking about other people.

3. She seldom chooses her words carefully when speaking about other people.

4. The mother asks her son if they been feeding him in the prison.

4. The mother asks her son if they been feeding him in the prison.

4. The mother asks her son if they been feeding him well in the prison.

5. I spilled/spilt coffee on my new shirt.

5. I spilled/spilt coffee on my new shirt.

5. I accidentally spilled/spilt coffee on my new shirt.

6. The doctor told him to breathe and hold his breath.

6. The doctor told him to breathe and hold his breath.

6. The doctor told him to breathe deeply and hold his breath.

7. He had to brake to avoid a dog.

7. He had to brake to avoid a dog.

7. He had to brake suddenly to avoid a dog.

8. The kung fu master broke the brick with his bare hand.

8. The kung fu master broke the brick with his bare hand.

8. The kung fu master broke the brick easily with his bare hand.

9. His parents were angry that he behaved that night.

9. His parents were angry that he behaved that night.

9. His parents were angry that he behaved badly that night.

10. The unknown disease is spreading and is feared by the people.

10. The unknown disease is spreading and is feared by the people.

10. The unknown disease is spreading fast and is greatly feared by the people.

The following adverbs are modifiers that modify verbs in the sentences:
badly, briefly, lately, narrowly, proudly, repeatedly, softly, steadily, swiftly, tightly

Place each one in the correct position in the sentence:

11. We walked and within minutes we were at the station.

11. We walked and within minutes we were at the station.

11. We walked swiftly and within minutes we were at the station.

12. The other day we spoke on the phone.

12. The other day we spoke on the phone.

12. The other day we spoke briefly on the phone.

13. She put her head on her arms and cried.

13. She put her head on her arms and cried.

13. She put her head on her arms and cried softly.

14. Where have you been?

14. Where have you been?

14. Where have you been lately?

15. They pushed him down and kicked him.

15. They pushed him down and kicked him.

15. They pushed him down and kicked him repeatedly.

16. The four of them jogged up the hill.

16. The four of them jogged up the hill.

16. The four of them jogged steadily up the hill.

17. Her grandma hurt her leg in the fall.

17. Her grandma hurt her leg in the fall.

17. Her grandma hurt her leg badly in the fall.

18. He welcomed his son at the airport by hugging him.

18. He welcomed his son at the airport by hugging him.

18. He welcomed his son at the airport by hugging him tightly.

19. She displayed her degree certificate to her parents.

19. She displayed her degree certificate to her parents.

19. She proudly displayed her degree certificate to her parents.

20. The team was beaten in their first away game.

20. The team was beaten in their first away game.

20. The team was narrowly beaten in their first away game.
