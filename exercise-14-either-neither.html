Exercise 14 - Either and Neither

  • Exercise 13 - Indirect Speech
  • Exercise 15 - Passive Voice

Fill in the blank with the correct answer given in bracket.

Click each question to see the answer.

1. Either of your parents _____ (has, have) me your teacher.

1. Either of your parents _____ (has, have) me your teacher.

1. Either of your parents has met your teacher.

2. Do either of you _____ (know, knows) where the TV remote is?

2. Do either of you _____ (know, knows) where the TV remote is?

2. Do either of you know where the TV remote is?

3. Either of them _____ (is, are) surely the culprit.

3. Either of them _____ (is, are) surely the culprit.

3. Either of them is surely the culprit.

4. We have seen a couple of houses, but neither _____ (was, were) suitable.

4. We have seen a couple of houses, but neither _____ (was, were) suitable.

4. We have seen a couple of houses, but neither was suitable.

5. Neither of them _____ (is, are) interested in playing with us.

5. Neither of them _____ (is, are) interested in playing with us.

5. Neither of them is interested in playing with us.

6. Neither of the children _____ (was, were) told to tell lies.

6. Neither of the children _____ (was, were) told to tell lies.

6. Neither of the children was told to tell lies.

7. Either of the two cars _____ (is, are) red in colour.

7. Either of the two cars _____ (is, are) red in colour.

7. Either of the two cars is red in colour..

8. Neither of the two girls _____ (know, knows) where their brother is.

8. Neither of the two girls _____ (know, knows) where their brother is.

8. Neither of the two girls knows where their brother is.

9. You can ride either _____ (bicycle, bicycles).

9. You can ride either _____ (bicycle, bicycles).

9. You can ride either bicycle.

10. There is a window at either ______ (side, sides) of the room.

10. There is a window at either ______ (side, sides) of the room.

10. There is a window at either side of the room.

11. Neither parents _____ (care, cares) how their children behave.

11. Neither parents _____ (care, cares) how their children behave.

11. Neither parents cares how their children behave.

12. Neither you nor he _____ (is. are) to be blamed for what happened.

12. Neither you nor he _____ (is. are) to be blamed for what happened.

12. Neither you nor he is to be blamed for what happened.

13. I don't eat a lot and my wife doesn't _____ (either, neither).

13. I don't eat a lot and my wife doesn't _____ (either, neither).

13. I don't eat a lot and my wife doesn't either.

14. Your father isn't happy with what you did, _____ is your mother (either, neither).

14. Your father isn't happy with what you did, _____ is your mother (either, neither).

14. Your father isn't happy with what you did, neither is your mother.

15. “You haven't gone to see the movie?” “I haven't _____ (either, neither).”

15. “You haven't gone to see the movie?” “I haven't _____ (either, neither).”

15. “You haven't gone to see the movie?” “I haven't either.”
