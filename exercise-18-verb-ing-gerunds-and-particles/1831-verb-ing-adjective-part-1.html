18.3.1 Verb + -ing (Adjective) Part 1

  • Print
  • Email

  • 18.3.2 Verb + -ing (Adjective) Part 2

Use the answer words as provided. Each word can only be used once.

Click the sentences to see the answers.

(excited, exciting)

1. Many are attracted to the _____ new magazine.

1. Many are attracted to the _____ new magazine.

1. Many are attracted to the exciting new magazine.

2. He was all _____ about his new car.

2. He was all _____ about his new car.

2. He was all excited about his new car.

(interested, interesting)

3. They have chosen some _____ topics for discussion.

3. They have chosen some _____ topics for discussion.

3. They have chosen some interesting topics for discussion.

4. She's always been _____ in other people.

4. She's always been _____ in other people.

4. She's always been interested in other people.

(annoyed, annoying)

5. His attitude made me extremely _____.

5. His attitude made me extremely _____.

5. His attitude made me extremely annoyed.

6. Charles has an _____ habit of interrupting.

6. Charles has an _____ habit of interrupting.

6. Charles has an annoying habit of interrupting.

(amazed, amazing)

7. I am still _____ that she won first prize.

7. I am still _____ that she won first prize.

7. I am still amazed that she won first prize.

8. That really was a truly _____ achievement

8. That really was a truly _____ achievement

8. That really was a truly amazing achievement.

(exhausted, exhausting)

9. I was _____ from the day's work.

9. I was _____ from the day's work.

9. I was exhausted from the day's work.

10. I will always remember that really _____ journey.

10. I will always remember that really _____ journey.

10. I will always remember that really exhausting journey.

(lost, losing)

11. By this time we were completely _____.

11. By this time we were completely _____.

11. By this time we were completely lost.

12. The _____ team had a player sent off earlier in the match.

12. The _____ team had a player sent off earlier in the match.

12. The losing team had a player sent off earlier in the match.

(worried, worrying)

13. She was _____ sick about her son.

13. She was _____ sick about her son.

13. She was worried sick about her son.

14. The report highlights a _____ trend towards a sharp increase in violent crimes.

14. The report highlights a _____ trend towards a sharp increase in violent crimes.

14. The report highlights a worrying trend towards a sharp increase in violent crimes.

(tired, tiring)

15. He felt _____ enough to go to sleep standing up.

15. He felt _____ enough to go to sleep standing up.

15. He felt tired enough to go to sleep standing up.

16. We have all had a very _____ day.

16. We have all had a very _____ day.

16. We have all had a very tiring day.

(disappointed, disappointing)

17. It was a terribly _____ film.

17. It was a terribly _____ film.

17. It was a terribly disappointing film.

18. I felt very _____ when we lost.

18. I felt very _____ when we lost.

18. I felt very disappointed when we lost.

(threatened, threatening)

19. They were very resentful of his _____ behavior.

19. They were very resentful of his _____ behavior.

19. They were very resentful of his threatening behavior.

20. Many species of fish are now _____ with extinction.

20. Many species of fish are now _____ with extinction.

20. Many species of fish are now threatened with extinction.
